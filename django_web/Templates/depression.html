Dealing With Depression

Getting Help

It is important to remember that you are not alone in your struggles. Reaching out to the friends and family is a great first step to addressing your feelings.

If you feel like more professional help is necessary try reaching out to the following links:
National Suicide prevention Hotline: 1-800-273-8255
SAMHSA'S National Helpline: 1-800-622-4357
Behavorial Health Treatment Service Locator

Self-Help

Sometimes some self-reflection is necessary to realize that you are not ok.
Here are some methods to stave off feelings of depression:

Exercise: Exercising at least once per day can go great lengths to improve your mood. Try to introduce around 1 hour of exercise into your daily routine and you will start to feel better.

Sleep: Getting good sleep is the most important way to get rid of depression. Everyone has different needs so try and figure out how much sleep you need to feel good, and prioritize getting it.

Taking Breaks: Setting time aside for yourself each day is crucial for dealing with depression. Taking a break from work to do something that you love will be sure to improve your mood.

Diet: A balanced diet is important for determining how you feel. Make sure that you are getting enough food, but avoid binge eating. Eating some healthier foods, like fruits and vegetables, can never hurt.
